The Great Wall of China is a military defense project that was built over 2,000 years by various Chinese dynasties
  • Origins: The Great Wall was originally built in the 3rd century BCE by Emperor Qin Shi Huang to unite China after the Warring States Period. The wall was built to keep out Mongolian raiders and was made of compacted earth.
  • Expansion:The Great Wall was expanded and strengthened by successive dynasties. The Ming Dynasty (1368â€“1664) rebuilt the wall to protect China from the Mongols, who had conquered China during the Yuan Dynasty.
  • Rebuilding:The Great Wall was rebuilt in the 1980s as a symbol of Chinese history, culture, and national pride.
  • Purpose:The Great Wall was built to fortify China's northern border and was the site of many battles. It was also used as a guide for trade routes.
  • Features: The Great Wall includes walls, horse tracks, watch towers, shelters, fortresses, and passes.
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site: The Great Wall was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List in 1987.
  • Symbol of Chinese power: The Great Wall is a symbol of Chinese power and independence.
