• Size: The statue was over 40 feet tall.
  • Material: The statue was made of ivory plates and gold panels on a wooden framework.
  • Throne: The statue sat on a cedarwood throne decorated with gold, ivory, ebony, and precious stones.
  • Location: The statue was erected in the Temple of Zeus in the Altis, a religious sanctuary in Olympia, Greece.
  • Sculptor: The statue was created by the Greek sculptor Phidias around 435 BC. The statue was lost and destroyed before the end of the 6th century AD. The exact date and circumstances of its destruction are unclear. Some say the statue was destroyed when Emperor Theodosius II banned the Olympic Games in 426 AD and closed the temple. Others say it was destroyed in a fire at the palace where it was located in 462 AD.The site of Olympia is now a UNESCO World Heritage site. The archaeological site includes the remains of the Temple of Zeus, and the workshop of Phidias.
